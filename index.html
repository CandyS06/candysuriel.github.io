About Me

Hi, I’m Candy Suriel, an islander from the Dominican Republic now
navigating life in the United States.


My journey into the world of travel began during a university
exchange program in Brazil, where I embraced the challenge of living
independently in a vast country and immersing myself in a language
that was new to me (although it was super similar).


This experience ignited my passion for exploration and cultural
exchange, teaching me resilience and adaptability.

Professionally

Industrial Engineer with a MSc in Manufacturing and Mechanical
and a minor in Quality.


I’ve built a career in Quality and Regulatory within the medical
device industry.


I have successfully implemented Quality Management Systems
(QMS) that ensure compliance with industry standards and
regulatory requirements.


My role has involved leading problem-solving initiatives aimed at
identifying and addressing quality issues, ultimately enhancing
product safety and efficacy.


I have also developed and managed processes to ensure adherence
to applicable standards and regulations, including ISO 13485 and
FDA regulations. This includes conducting audits, facilitating
training, and promoting a culture of continuous improvement
across teams.


When I’m not working, you can find me planning my next
adventure or reflecting on past travels, each one shaping my
perspective on life.


I have a deep interest in health and wellness, constantly
seeking to expand my knowledge and apply it to my daily life.


Additionally, I’m passionate about giving back to my
community; I actively look for volunteering opportunities and
events that align with my values, allowing me to support causes
that matter to me.

My Interests

I am passionate about supporting organizations that make a
meaningful impact on the lives of cancer patients and their
families.


My favorite organizations are:

  • St. Jude Children’s Research Hospital

  • American Red Cross


These organizations not only provide vital resources but also

I encourage everyone to consider donating to these causes, as

and ultimately save lives."
